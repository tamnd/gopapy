## [0.2.10] - 2026-04-27

Phase 1.3 of the parse-every-Python roadmap
(`~/notes/Spec/1200/1238_gopapy_every_python_roadmap.md`).

### Fixed

- **Soft keywords as keyword-argument names.** The call-args path
  rejected `name=value` whenever `name` was `match`, `case`, or
  `type`. Those are *soft* keywords in CPython — they only act as
  keywords inside their specific syntactic positions (the `match`
  statement header, the `type` alias statement) and are ordinary
  identifiers everywhere else. The parser was treating them as fully
  reserved, so `self.subTest(case=case, ...)`, `self.subTest(match=
  ...)`, `mock(match='...')`, and friends fell out of the kwarg
  branch and produced "expected ), got =" at the `=`. New
  `parser.isSoftKeyword` is consulted alongside `isReservedKeyword`
  in the call-args lookahead. Eleven CPython 3.14 stdlib files now
  parse that did not before — `test_codecs`, `test_dataclasses`,
  `test_exception_group`, `test_math`, `test_named_expressions`,
  `test_regrtest`, `test_str`, `test_super`, `test_tokenize`,
  `test_turtle`, `test_type_params`, plus `datetimetester`. Stdlib
  count: **1810 / 1842**, down from 32 failures (was 43).

### Added

- `tests/grammar/089_softkw_kwarg.py` covers `case=`/`match=`/`type=`
  as kwarg names in plain calls, in calls combined with `*args` and
  `**kwargs`, and as the `case=case` shadowing pattern that triggered
  the original failure.

### Changed

- CLI version bumped to `0.2.10`.
